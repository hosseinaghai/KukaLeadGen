# KUKA Robotics & Automation Offering for Industrial Manufacturers

KUKA's sales proposition for industrial manufacturers is built on three integrated pillars. The account manager should be able to discuss any pillar in isolation, but the full value comes from bundling all three into a long-term automation partnership.

---

## Pillar 1 — Robot Systems & Integration

**What it is:** Turnkey robotic work cells and production systems — from single-robot stations to fully automated production lines — designed, installed, and commissioned for the customer's specific application.

**What we do:**
- Assess the customer's production line and recommend the right robot family (KR AGILUS, KR CYBERTECH, KR IONTEC, KR QUANTEC, KR FORTEC, or collaborative LBR iisy / LBR iiwa)
- Design the cell layout with peripherals (grippers, sensors, safety fencing, vision systems)
- Program and commission the robot using KUKA's KR C5 controller and iiQKA.OS operating system
- Integrate with existing PLC / MES / ERP systems
- Perform process-specific validation (cycle time, quality, OEE targets)

**Who buys this:** The decision-maker varies by company size. In SME manufacturers (<100 FTE) this is the CEO/owner or Technical Director. In mid-sized companies (100-500 FTE) it is typically the Head of Production, VP Operations, or Plant Manager.

**Pricing model:** Capex per cell (typically €80K-€500K depending on robot payload, peripherals, and application complexity) plus optional leasing/financing through KUKA Financial Services.

**Why companies need it:** Labour shortages in manufacturing are acute across Europe; cycle-time consistency and quality repeatability from robots outperform manual operations; total cost of ownership for a robot cell breaks even in 12-24 months at typical utilisation rates.

---

## Pillar 2 — Software & Digital Services

**What it is:** KUKA's software ecosystem that maximises robot uptime, enables remote monitoring, and allows the customer to program, simulate, and optimise without deep robotics expertise.

**What we do:**
- Deploy iiQKA.OS2 operating system for intuitive robot operation
- Provide KUKA.Sim for offline programming and simulation (validate cycle times before building the cell)
- Enable KUKA smartPRODUCTION for real-time production monitoring, OEE dashboards, and predictive maintenance alerts
- Set up KUKA Xpert remote diagnostics for fault resolution without on-site visits
- Offer digital twin consulting to model entire production lines

**Who benefits:** Production engineers, maintenance teams, and operations managers. Reduces unplanned downtime by 25-40% on average.

**Pricing model:** Annual subscription per robot (€3K-€8K/robot/year depending on package) plus one-time setup/training.

**Why companies need it:** Most SME manufacturers operate robots with basic teach-pendant programming and reactive maintenance. Digital services unlock 15-30% more effective capacity from the same hardware.

---

## Pillar 3 — Service, Maintenance & Training

**What it is:** Lifecycle services that keep robot systems running at peak performance — from spare parts and preventive maintenance contracts to KUKA College training programmes for operators and engineers.

**Who buys this:** Companies that already have KUKA robots installed and want to maximise uptime and build internal competence.

**What we do:**
- Preventive and predictive maintenance programmes (KUKA Care packages: Basic / Smart / Premium)
- 24/7 remote support hotline
- On-site corrective maintenance and emergency response
- Spare-parts guarantee contracts (next-business-day delivery)
- KUKA College training: operator certification, programming (KRL), maintenance, and advanced process training
- Retrofit and upgrade programmes for older KR C4-based systems to KR C5 / iiQKA

**Pricing model:** Annual service contracts (€5K-€25K/robot/year depending on tier and SLA), training priced per participant per course (€1.5K-€4K/course).

**Why companies need it:** Unplanned downtime costs manufacturers €5K-€20K per hour in lost production. A preventive maintenance contract typically pays for itself after avoiding a single extended breakdown event.

## How the 3 Pillars Combine in the Sales Pitch

| Prospect profile | Lead with | Why |
|------------------|-----------|-----|
| Greenfield — no robots yet, labour-shortage pain | Pillar 1 (robot systems) | Their pain is hiring and consistency; start with one cell, expand from success |
| Existing KUKA fleet, low utilisation / high downtime | Pillar 3 (service) + Pillar 2 (digital) | Unlock more output from hardware they already own; Pillar 1 expansion follows |
| Existing competitor robots (ABB, Fanuc, Yaskawa), considering fleet expansion | Pillar 1 (robot systems) + Pillar 2 (digital) | Win the next cell; iiQKA ease-of-use and digital services differentiate vs. legacy competitor platforms |
| Mid-size manufacturer with 5+ KUKA robots, growing | All 3 pillars as a partnership agreement | Bundle discount + dedicated account manager; anchor the relationship before the fleet grows further |

## Estimated Annual Deal Value (Rough Sizing)

A useful heuristic for the account manager:

- **Pillar 1:** €120K-€400K per cell (one-time), 1-3 cells per mid-size project
- **Pillar 2:** €5K × number of robots in fleet per year
- **Pillar 3:** €12K × number of robots in fleet per year (blended across tiers)

So a mid-size manufacturer with 8 KUKA robots buying one additional cell plus full service and digital package represents on the order of **€250K-€500K in first-year revenue** and **€136K/year recurring** thereafter.
